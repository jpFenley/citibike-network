New York City is home to the nation's largest bike share program, Citi Bike. Citi Bike collects and shares data on each ride, including the station and time that each ride started and ended. Finding trends in this data to identify popular routes based on the time of day and day of the week can help Citi Bike riders determine which routes to take and help Citi Bike determine how their bikes should be allocated.

This interactive tool explores the trends in Manhattan's rides on Citi Bike in June 2019. Step through a guided exploration of these rides by pressing the "Begin" button above. For a self-guided exploration, press the "Jump to Interactive" button in the bottom left at any time.

This is Manhattan. It is home to over 1.6 million people.

In 2013 Citi Bike released its bikeshare program. The program has grown since, becoming the largest in the United States, and the second largest in the world.

There are currently 419 stations in Manhattan.

There are around 12,000 bicycles across all these stations.

With 419 stations that means there are 175,142 possible rides.

In June of 2019, we saw 109,674 of these possible ride combinations.


However, some of these rides are way more popular than others.

The 4 most common rides of all time occur at Governor's Island, between Yankee Ferry Terminal, Soissons Landing , and Picnic Point stations.

The reason there only appears to be two rides shown is because the rides go to and from the same station.

During the morning we can see Midtown and the Hudson Yards being the most popular ride locations. These happen to be some of the most common places to work in the city.

Due to how popular certain stations and rides are at this time, many users encounter empty stations without bikes. Citi Bike has been attempting to relocate bikes to match this demand, but the issue is still present.

During the afternoon Central Park is a common ride location.

Hudson yards and Midtown are still popular routes, most likely caused by the same morning commuters heading home.

At night, Central Park has practically no rides as it closes after 1am.

Chelsea and the East Village are the most popular ride locations. These locations are where a lot of nightlife and bars are located.

During weekdays the most popular routes are very similar to the most popular during the morning and afternoon commutes. It is mostly commuting to and from work at the Hudson Yards and Midtown stations.

During weekends almost all rides appear to be leisurely. The most common paths are along the Hudson river in Hudson Yards and through Central Park. There is very limited riding through the city itself.
